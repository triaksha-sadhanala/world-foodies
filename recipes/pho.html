Pho (non-authentic but easier)

One of Vietnam's most popular and loved dishes, and it'll soon be one of yours as soon as you taste it. So, why wait when you have the chance to get started? Let's go!

Ingredients

For preparation:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 shallots - diced
  • 1 bunch green onions - chopped, green and white parts divided
  • 4 cloves garlic - minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh ginger - minced
  • 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth - or water
  • 2 whole star anise
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce

For assembly:

  • 1 pound flat rice noodles - dried or fresh
  • 1 pound beef - flank steak, chuck roast, brisket, sirloin (sliced thin against the grain)
  • Sliced red or white onions
  • Fresh cilantro - chopped

Instructions

  1. Cook the mirepoix. Add your olive oil and shallots to a medium stockpot over medium heat. Cook your shallots for around 5 minutes, making sure to stir often. Add the white parts of the onion, minced garlic, and ginger. Stir well, combine, and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add your pre-made broth/stock. Your store brought broth/stock to a pot and simmer. Then add your flavors. You can add 1-2 whole star anise, soy sauce, and or fish sauce to the broth. Cover it and leave it to simmer for 15-20 minutes.

How to assemble the bowl:

  1. Slice your meat. It is best if it is thinly sliced. If you going through trouble slicing the meat, you could put it in the freezer for around 10 minutes. This should give you an easier time.
  2. Noodles first. Use a large bowl, then divide between as many bowls as desired.
  3. Add the raw meat. Yes, RAW. Directly add the meat on top of the rice noodles. Assuming your broth is boiling, and not just luke-warm, the meat should be fully cooked.
  4. Add the broth. Make sure it's extremely hot and ladle some directly on the meat.

Tips:

  • Add lime juice if you'd like your pho to be sourer, or Sriracha, hoisin sauce, or fresh chili if you're going for something spicier.
  • Higher quality serves for better pho, so try to get the best ingredient quality possible when cooking!
