Tahini Miso Ramen

This recipe is a dreamy, creamy, and most definitely addictive noodle recipe that you'll want to keep coming back for.

Ingredients

For your mushrooms and tofu:

  • 1 14-ounce (396g) block of extra firm tofu
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 Tablespoon soy sauce, divided
  • 1 Tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 pound (450g) cremini mushrooms
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the miso broth:

  • 2 Tablespoons sesame oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 Tablespoon ginger, freshly grated
  • 1 Tablespoon white miso paste
  • 6 cups (1,5L) vegetable broth
  • ¼ cup dried mushrooms
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 4 3-ounce (4x85g) ramen noodles, fresh or dry
  • 2 teaspoons rice vinegar
  • 1 Tablespoon hot chili oil

Instructions

  1. When starting to make your Tahini Miso Ramen, start by making your Crispy Tofu. Then you would want to saute the mushrooms.
  2. Next you would need to start on the miso broth. Meanwhile, when waiting for the broth to boil, then prepare the soft-boiled egg. It will take around 6 minutes to boil.
  3. Add the noodles in and cook according to what the package says. (Most ramen noodles would take 3 to 4 minutes to cook.) Add the final seasoning to the broth and discard the dried mushrooms.

Important note:

If your noodles take longer than 6 minutes to cook, get the boiled egg ready before adding the noodles. But if your noodles don't take a long time to prepare, put your noodles in and then get your soft-boiled egg ready.

How to assemble the bowl:

  1. Divide the noodles into the bowls and then ladle your miso ramen broth.
  2. Add your crispy tofu and sauteed mushrooms. If you choose to make sure to add your soft-boiled egg at this step.
  3. Now garnish with your desired seasonings.

Tips:

  • Experiment by topping with thinly sliced scallions, freshly chopped cilantro, sesame seeds, chili flakes, sweet corn, Sichuan peppers, dry-roasted Nori seaweed, fried garlic, chili oil, or, for a different flavor, try kimchi!
