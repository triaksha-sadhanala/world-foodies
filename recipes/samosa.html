Onion Samosa

The onions and masala mix in your mouth, creating a tapestry of delicious flavors, especially with the crunchy layer texture of fried dough.

Ingredients

For Pastry Sheets:

  • 1 cup wheat flour
  • ½ all-purpose flour(maida)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp of oil

Onion Stuffing:

  • ⅛ cup of oil
  • 4 large onions (Sliced)
  • Salt to taste
  • ⅛ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • ½ coriander powder
  • ½ cumin powder
  • ⅛ tsp garam masala
  • 1 pinch chaat masala

For Maida Paste:

  • 2 tbsp maida paste
  • Water

Instructions

For Pastry Sheets:

  1. Mix up the wheat flour, maida, salt, and oil together in a bowl. Start kneading the dough to make a smooth, soft dough, while adding water along the way.
  2. Close up the bowl with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  3. After it's done resting, knead the dough again and cut it into equal pieces.
  4. Turn each piece of dough into a ball and dust with enough flour until you can spread it out into a thin elongated disc.
  5. Cut a rectangle out of it and prepare the rest of the pieces.

For Onion Filling:

  1. Heat up oil in a pan and drop the sliced onions in let them fry with salt until transparent
  2. Add in the turmeric powder, chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and chaat masala
  3. Fry the mixture until the smell of raw onions is not there anymore then the filling is ready

For putting the filling in the sheets:

  1. Fold the sheet from one corner to the other and vice versa three times making sure you can hold it and there is a gap to put in the filling at one edge
  2. Put the filling in
  3. Mix up the maida and water to make the paste
  4. Apply the paste to the corners of the remaining sheets
  5. Fold it up and secure it by pressing it once

For frying them:

  1. Prepare all the rest of them
  2. Heat oil in something that you can deep-fry in. When the oil is hot, put in about 2-3 samosas.
  3. Turn it over often to cook both sides and cook until golden brown
  4. Take them out of the oil and serve them up hot

Tips:

  • Instead of making the dough from scratch, you can buy torrential flatbread, place the topping inside, and then fry for convenience.
