Piroshki(Russian meat pies)

Simple, easy, and absolutely one of the most delicious spins on chicken curry! This recipe consists of tender, char-grilled chicken, simmered in a splendid, aromatic sauce.

Ingredients

For dough:

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • About 1 ½ teaspoons of baking yeast (Red Star Platinum Superior Baking Yeast; 1 packet)
  • ½ of salt
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of warm water (Add more if needed)

For meat filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 lb of lean ground beef
  • 2-3 cloves (Pressed)
  • 1 teaspoon table salt
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. To start to make the dough, combine flour, yeast, and salt in a mixing bowl
  2. Add in vegetable oil and water, then mix until smooth (If the dough is too dry, add more water; too wet, add more flour.)
  3. Cover the dough in a little bit of vegetable oil and place it back in the bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp kitchen towel and put it in a warm place to let the dough rise for about 30-60 minutes.
  4. While the dough is rising, prepare the filling. Heat olive oil in a pan, skillet, or Dutch oven over medium high heat
  5. Add in the meat, garlic, and table salt. Let the meat start to cook and start breaking apart with a wooden spoon until cooked through. Then transfer it into a bowl and add the cooked rice in and mix it up.
  6. Once the dough has risen or doubled in size, take it out and put it on a lightly floured surface and knead into a ball, which should take 1-2 minutes. Flatten the ball into a disc and cut it into 12 equal pieces like how you would cut a pizza. Roll each of the pieces into a ball.
  7. To start making the pie, take a ball of dough and flatten it in your hands, then put the filling in the center and fold the dough in half over it. Pinch the edges of the dough together to create a sort of half-moon shape. Then, gently flatten it, making sure the sealed crimps are on the flat side.
  8. Once the pies are formed, heat 1 inch of oil in a pan or skillet.
  9. To cook, simply drop them in the oil when ready and cook until they are golden brown, which usually takes 3-4 minutes. Then, flip and do the same thing. When done, put in a bowl with paper towels to remove the excess oil. Then, proceed to continue with the remaining ones.
  10. Serve at warm or room temperature.

Tips:

  • If you're full and still have space in the fridge, cover them in a plastic container and store them with a plastic cover, then reheat them in the oven or the microwave. Piroshki can stay in the fridge for 5 to 6 days.
