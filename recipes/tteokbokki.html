Tteokbokki

A delicious, spicy, and flavourful meal with a burst of flavor with each spoonful. It's a perfect dinner for a hungry family.

Ingredients

  • 350g / 12 ounces Korean rice cakes, separated
  • 150g / 5.3 ounces Korean fish cakes, rinsed over hot water & cut into bite-size pieces
  • 2 cups Korean soup stock (dried kelp and dried anchovy stock), use this recipe
  • 60g / 2 ounces onion, thinly sliced

Tteokbokki sauce(mix these in a bowl):

  • 3 Tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp raw sugar
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp gochujang (Korean chili flakes)

For garnish or extra:

  • 1 tsp roasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 stalk green onion, finely chopped

Instructions

  1. Soak rice cakes in warm water for 10 minutes (if already fresh, no need, but if from the fridge make sure to soak them to make them taste better)
  2. Boil the soup stock in a shallow pot over medium heat and dissolve the tteokbokki sauce by stirring. Once the seasoned stock is boiling, add rice cakes, fish cakes, and onions. Boil until everything is fully cooked
  3. Add green onions, sesame oil, and sesame seeds, then quickly stir. Preferably served warm.

Tips:

  • With any leftover sauce, adding cooked ramen or rice can save food and make a more filling meal if you are not satisfied.
